Things to do

Destinations

The Blarney Stone

Kissing the stone endows the kisser with the ‘gift of the gab’ (great eloquence or skill at flattery)

Leap Castle

There are many haunted castles around Ireland, but none are as infamous as Leap Castle in County Offaly.

Carrauntoohil

Located in County Kerry, Carrauntoohil is the highest peak in Ireland. It measures 1,038 metres (3,406 ft) high and is the central peak of the MacGillycuddy’s Reeks range. The mountain isn’t just big, it is also beautiful, and this makes it one of the best things to do in Ireland for hiking enthusiasts.

Grafton Street

This is Dublin's shopping heartland, Grafton Street is alive with buskers, flower-sellers, and performance artists. You will also find countless places to stop off and simply watch the world meander by.

Wicklow Mountains

The focal point of the Wicklow Mountains is the lake of Glendalough which has to be Ireland’s most-beautiful lake! Glendalough, or the ‘Valley of Two Lakes’, is one of Ireland’s most prominent monastic sites, nestled in the heart of the Wicklow Mountains National Park. Nicknamed ‘the garden of Ireland’, Wicklow is a nature-lover’s paradise of rolling meadows, vast lakes, and hillsides carpeted in purple heather.

Cliffs of Moher

With up to one million visitors annually, these cliffs are Ireland’s most-visited natural attraction. Standing at an impressive 214m (702 feet) at their highest point, they stretch for 8 kilometres (5 miles) along the Atlantic coast of County Clare in the west of Ireland. On a clear day, from these impressive cliffs, you can see as far as the Aran Islands and Galway Bay. It is one of the best things to do in Ireland if you enjoy scenic walks and incredible natural beauty!

Food and Drink

1 / 10
Irish stew is a one-pot dish commonly made from lamb, mutton, or beef and root vegetables grown locally. As with all traditional Irish recipes, the ingredients can vary from place to place and family to family.
2 / 10
Irish soda bread is a simple, moist bread created using only four basic ingredients (bicarbonate of soda, buttermilk, flour, and salt).
3 / 10
A Boxty is a potato pancake made from cooked mashed potato, grated raw potato, buttermilk, flour, and salt. Boxtys can be paired with almost anything and are used in a variety of different ways, the most common is alongside eggs and bacon in the morning.
4 / 10
Considered the holy grail of mashed potatoes, Colcannon consists of mashed potatoes, kale, green onions, milk or cream and lots of butter.
5 / 10
Barmbrack is a sweet Irish bread made with dried fruit (usually sultanas and raisins), and is the perfect accompaniment to a pot of tea.
6 / 10
The perk of being on an island is access to a variety of fresh shellfish. There are Connemara clams, prawns in Dublin Bay, and Molly Malone’s famous cockles and mussels. Galway hossts the oldest oyster festival in the world, celebrating oyster-inspired dishes.
7 / 10
Known as the national drink of Ireland, Guinness is well-loved across the country and world over. The dark liquid is almost creamy in texture with a hoppy bitterness and tasting notes of coffee, chocolate, and herbs.
8 / 10
Irish Cider, known as Bulmers in the Irish Republic but referred to as Magners everywhere in the world, this cider is probably the best in Ireland. It’s made from 17 kinds of Irish apples and when you drink it you can imagine yourself in an orchard on the Emerald Isle.
9 / 10
Irish whiskeys are typically distilled using unmalted barley and triple distilled so generally produce a smooth and sweet flavor profile.
10 / 10
Irish coffee is made in a warm glass and uses brown sugar, Irish whiskey, coffee, and fresh layered cream.
